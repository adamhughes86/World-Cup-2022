A report on where the goal scorers from the Mens World Cup 2022 play football

By Adam Hughes

I was interested in where the goal scorers for the World Cup play their regular football and couldn't find out the information easily. At the same time I was also looking for a project to improve some data skills so this felt like a good mini project to work on.

This report contains tables showing the leagues and teams who registered the most goals scored in the World Cup 2022. It also contains a Sankey diagram showing every goal scorer (excluding own goals), and which teams, countries, and leagues they play in. The data has been collated from the official Fifa website, Google, and Wikipedia

Using the dataset and Sankey diagram you can analyse and make some interesting observations for instance: PSG by far has the most goals by their players (18 goals) and this is helped because they had the top two goal scorers, Mbappe (8 goals) and Messi (7 goals). PSG also registered 66% of the goals (18 goals) by players in the top French league (27 goals).

Another observation is that the Premier League is the league with the most goals (37 goals) by players within it and this is made up of 25 players representing a wide selection of teams (12 teams). There was an average of 1.52 goals scored by players in the premier league compared with 1.45 across the whole dataset
